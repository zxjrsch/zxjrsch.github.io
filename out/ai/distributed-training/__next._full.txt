The second piece of data which require storage are the activation of each layers, since these are used to compute the  gradient of weights in the layer to which the activations are the inputs during forward pass, as we have already seen. Activation can be released whenever the weight gradients they are associated with are computed. The third piece of data that is stored are activation gradients. They are used to compute gradients during backpropagation through the next layer (or operation), and can be released after computing activation and weight gradients in the next layer (or operation). The fourth piece of data are weight gradients, with a lifetime from produced until the corresponding weights are updated. The fifth type of data are associated with the optimization algorithm which updates the weight using the gradients. In the Adam optimizer, we need to keep track of the exponential moving average of the weight and the square of each weight.
